About Me

Hi, I’m Navya! I am a senior at Milton High School interested in materials science and polymer chemistry for sustainability. I aim to study computational materials science in college, designing polymers for biomaterials, fabric, and electronics. I am fascinated by the potential of novel materials to minimize our environmental footprint, and I aim to explore this space using synthetic chemistry and mathematical models.

Recently, I worked on a project where I developed a machine-learning based virtual screening method to filter and analyze biodegradable polyesters. Previously, I worked on computational modeling and biochemical analysis for the early diagnosis and treatment of various diseases. Check out my portfolio!

I also enjoy organizing various human rights initiatives through Milton’s chapter of Amnesty International, and you can see our projects here! Some of my hobbies include reading fictional books (ex. A Thousand Splendid Suns, The Color Purple, Frankenstein), hiking (loved Zion and Olympic national parks!), and playing the violin.

AI-based System to Predict High Risk Pregnancies

- Investigated which factors contribute to a high risk pregnancy.

- Built a machine learning model to calculate a patient’s risk score based on demographic, clinical, and lab information.

- Compared several different methods, including linear regression, tree-based models, and neural networks, to see which produced the most interpretable and accurate results.

SupportED: Emotional and Physiological Analysis of Anorexia Warning Signs

- This project focused on the two facets of Anorexia progression: mental and physical manifestations.

- Given the high prevalence of this condition in adolescents and their social media presence, I conducted AI-based sentiment analysis on Instagram post captions to identify and quantify the relative importance of words and phrases indicative of Anorexia Nervosa

- For physiological monitoring, I determined the carbohydrate starvation level at which the gut microbiome deteriorates

- Presented at the 2023 Emory Southeastern Pediatric Research Conference [link] [program, pg 112]

- Georgia State Technology Competition (GASTC) 1st place in the state in the mobile apps category

Poster | Paper | Demo

Intellidose: Model and App to Calculate Personalized Antibiotic Dosages

- This project focused on calculating personalized antibiotic dosages to prevent antibiotic resistance using machine learning methods.

- I built machine learning models to compute the minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) of an antibiotic required to inhibit bacterial growth.

- I translated my results into a mobile app, where a clinician can input information about the patient, infection type, and materials on hand, and the app will compute a personalized dosage.

- Georgia State Technology Competition (GASTC) 1st place in the state in the Mobile Applications category

- Georgia Science and Engineering Fair Best 7th grade project

Slides | Demo

Modeling Neurodegeneration: The Effect of Oxidative Stress on Saccharomyces cerevisiae

- This project focused on modeling the mechanisms of neurodegenerative diseases, such as Alzheimer’s and Parkinsons, on Brewers yeast.

- Oxidative stress is a primary driver of neurodegeneration, so we modeled this process using a fenton reaction, which creates hydroxyl radicals

- We observed that higher concentrations of hydrogen peroxide linearly indicated greater neurodegeneration (with H. Choi and J. Gosukonda as part of the Georgia Governor's Honors Program 2023).
